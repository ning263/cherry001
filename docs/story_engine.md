# Story Engine

The story engine defines how a work is transformed into an experienceable narrative path.

## Core Idea

The engine should not produce ordinary summaries. It should identify the living pattern of a work:

- What human situation is being staged.
- What emotional promise pulls the reader forward.
- What narrative strategy makes the work memorable.

## Story Archetype

A story archetype is not a genre label. It is the deep human pattern that makes the work emotionally recognizable.

Examples:

| Work | Possible Archetype | Narrative Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Count of Monte Cristo | Revenge myth | A man returns from the abyss. |
| Ordinary World | Growth myth | The dignity of ordinary people. |

These examples are working hypotheses, not final canon.

## Emotional Promise

Each work should be understood through the emotional promise it offers the user.

Questions:

- What emotional journey does the work invite?
- What pain, desire, or conflict does it help the user inhabit?
- What kind of return does the user get after staying with the story?

## Narration Strategy

The narration strategy should decide:

- Where to begin.
- Which scenes must be preserved.
- Which causal links must not be skipped.
- Which emotional transitions require pacing.
- When discussion or interpretation should appear.

## Engine Output

The story engine may eventually produce structured outputs such as:

- Story nodes.
- Causal nodes.
- Emotional nodes.
- Scene cards.
- Discussion prompts.
- Interpretation boundaries.

The exact schema is still open.
