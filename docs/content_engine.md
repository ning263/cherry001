# Content Engine

The content engine defines how the system generates literary content that feels alive rather than like a generic AI summary.

## Problem

Ordinary AI summaries often fail because they move too quickly into abstraction.

They may tell the user what a book is "about" while skipping:

- Concrete scenes.
- Causal pressure.
- Emotional transitions.
- Character decisions.
- The reader's gradual discovery.

## Desired Content Shape

Good content should preserve:

- Scene: where the user can enter.
- Causality: why events unfold.
- Emotion: what changes internally.
- Tension: what remains unresolved.
- Openness: what should not be over-explained.

## Narration Before Analysis

The content should not sound like a compressed product analysis.

Before naming a theme, the narration should let the user feel why the theme matters. A good pattern is:

1. Start from the ordinary bad summary and reject it.
2. Search for the story archetype.
3. Enter the protagonist's situation through concrete scenes.
4. Preserve the causal chain.
5. Let the emotional question emerge.
6. Only then offer a provisional interpretation.

The voice can be conversational, but it should not become loose or shallow. Short lines are allowed when they create pacing and help the user feel the story pressure.

## Calibration Failure: Too Analytical

If content moves too quickly into labels such as "power", "authority", "trauma", or "interpretation", it may be accurate but emotionally premature.

Failure signs:

- The output reads like an essay outline.
- The protagonist is explained before the user has entered the protagonist's situation.
- Scenes are mentioned but not allowed to breathe.
- The archetype appears as a final label rather than a discovered pattern.
- The conclusion is correct but not earned.

## What To Avoid

Avoid content that:

- Starts with a big conclusion.
- Compresses characters into labels.
- Explains symbolism before experience.
- Treats one interpretation as final.
- Claims to replace reading.

## Content Priorities

Current priority order:

1. Story experience.
2. Causal coherence.
3. Emotional continuity.
4. Grounded interpretation.
5. Discussion after experience.

## Open Design Area

The content engine still needs clearer rules for:

- Different types of works.
- Long novels versus short works.
- Fiction versus non-fiction.
- Chinese and foreign classics.
- User-specific reading history.
- Post-listening or post-reading discussion.
